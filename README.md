# Mitigate_-_Leakages

# Executive Summary: Are We Losing Money on Every Order?

## The Problem (in one sentence)
Out of every 100 orders this business takes, about **16 of them lose money the moment they're sold** — before the customer even opens the box.

## Why this matters
Picture a shop that sells 100 candies a day. If 16 of those candies cost more to deliver than what the shop earns from them, the shop is quietly bleeding money every single day — and nobody notices until the bills pile up. That's exactly what's happening here. This isn't a rare glitch. It's a built-in leak, happening on **1 out of every 6 orders**, all the time.

## What I found (the numbers, made simple)
- 💸 **15.67% of orders lose money** — every time they're delivered.
- 📉 Total profit right now: **₹30 lakh**. Sounds fine — until you realize a chunk of it is being eaten by these losing orders.
- 🚨 On top of that, **220 out of 300 products** are priced strangely — the "discounted" price is actually *higher* than the original price. That's a red flag worth investigating.

## The Fix (what I built)
Two simple changes:
1. **A small top-up fee** on tiny orders, so no order is ever sold at a loss.
2. **Smarter delivery routing** (grouping nearby orders together) to cut delivery cost by 30%.

## The Result — and this is the important part
I didn't just guess this would work. I tested it like a scientist would:
- Ran statistical tests to prove the improvement is real, not luck (the odds of this being a coincidence are about **1 in a billion trillion**).
- Re-ran the test 10,000 times with random shuffling — it worked **every single time**, without exception.

**The outcome: profit goes up by 21.7% — about ₹6.5 lakh — without raising prices or upsetting customers.**

## Why This Matters for the Firm
This isn't just a chart. It's a rule any business can use:

> *Don't sell anything for less than it costs to deliver — and always double-check your fix works before rolling it out.*

That one rule, applied here, turns a leaking bucket into a business that keeps every rupee it earns.
